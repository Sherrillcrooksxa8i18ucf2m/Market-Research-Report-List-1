<p><p>Maleic anhydride adhesion promoters are used to improve the adhesion between two different materials or surfaces. There are various types of maleic anhydride adhesion promoters available in the market, including benzene oxidation method, C4 olefins, phthalic anhydride byproduction, N-butane oxidation, and others. </p><p>Benzene oxidation method involves the production of maleic anhydride through the oxidation of benzene. Phthalic anhydride byproduction is a process where maleic anhydride is obtained as a byproduct during the production of phthalic anhydride. N-butane oxidation involves the oxidation of N-butane to produce maleic anhydride. Other methods and markets also exist for maleic anhydride adhesion promoters.</p></p>
