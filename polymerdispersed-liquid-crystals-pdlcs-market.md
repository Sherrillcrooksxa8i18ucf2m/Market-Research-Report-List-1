<p><p>Polymer-Dispersed Liquid Crystals (PDLCs) are a type of smart material that consists of liquid crystal droplets dispersed in a polymer matrix. There are three main types: Scattering PDLCs, which scatter light when switched on/off; Nano-PDLCs, which use nanoparticles to improve switching speed and contrast ratio; and Polymer Network Liquid Crystals, which have a higher concentration of liquid crystal molecules in a polymer network. These PDLCs are used in applications such as privacy windows, smart glass, and displays due to their tunable light transmission properties.</p></p>
