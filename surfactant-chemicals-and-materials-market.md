<p><p>Surfactant chemicals and materials are classified into four main types: anionic, cationic, amphoteric, and nonionic. Anionic surfactants have a negatively charged head and are commonly used in household cleaning products. Cationic surfactants have a positively charged head and are often found in fabric softeners. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charged heads, making them versatile in various applications. Nonionic surfactants have no net charge and are gentle on the skin, making them suitable for use in personal care products.</p></p>
