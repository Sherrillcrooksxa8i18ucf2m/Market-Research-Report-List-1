<p><ul><li>Ionic</li><li>Nonionic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wool-protective agents can be classified into two main types based on their chemical properties: Ionic and Nonionic. Ionic wool-protective agents contain charged molecules that interact with the fibers of wool through electrostatic forces. Nonionic wool-protective agents do not have charged molecules and instead interact with the wool fibers through hydrogen bonding. Both types of agents are used to protect wool from damage, such as shrinking and felting, during washing and cleaning processes, and can help to maintain the quality and appearance of wool products.</p></p>
