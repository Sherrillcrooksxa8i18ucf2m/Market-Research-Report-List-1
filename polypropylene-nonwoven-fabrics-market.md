<p><ul><li>Spunbonded</li><li>Staples</li><li>Meltblown</li><li>Composite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polypropylene nonwoven fabrics are categorized into different market types based on their manufacturing process. Spunbonded fabrics are made by extruding continuous fibers which are then bonded together. Staples involve short fibers that are bonded together to form a fabric. Meltblown fabrics are created by blowing hot air through molten polymer to create fine fibers that are then cooled and collected on a conveyor belt. Composite fabrics are made by combining different types of fibers or layers to achieve specific properties or characteristics.</p></p>
