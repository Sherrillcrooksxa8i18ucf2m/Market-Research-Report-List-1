<p><p>Automotive safety devices can be categorized into two main types: active safety systems and passive safety systems. Active safety systems are designed to prevent accidents by assisting the driver in avoiding collisions, such as with features like automatic emergency braking and lane departure warning systems. On the other hand, passive safety systems are intended to protect occupants in the event of a crash, such as with features like airbags and seat belts. Both types of safety systems play a crucial role in reducing the number and severity of accidents on the road.</p></p>
