<p><p>Thermopile and Microbolometer are two types of Infrared (IR) detectors used for thermal imaging applications. Thermopile detectors are based on the Seebeck effect, where changes in temperature generate voltage, while Microbolometer detectors use changes in electrical resistance to measure temperature differences. Both detectors have their own advantages and disadvantages, with Microbolometer detectors being more sensitive and accurate, while Thermopile detectors are generally more cost-effective. The market for both types of IR detectors is growing due to increasing demand for thermal imaging technology in various industries.</p></p>
