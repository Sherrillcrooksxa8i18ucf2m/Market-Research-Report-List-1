<p><p>Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) devices are commonly categorized into two main types: Tabletop OCT Devices and Handheld OCT Devices. Tabletop OCT Devices are typically larger, stationary machines used in clinical settings for high-resolution imaging of biological tissues, including the eye. Handheld OCT Devices, on the other hand, are portable devices that offer greater mobility and flexibility for point-of-care imaging applications. Both types of OCT devices play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring various medical conditions, particularly in ophthalmology.</p></p>
