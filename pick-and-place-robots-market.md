<p><p>Pick-and-place robots are classified based on the number of axes they operate in. 2-axis robots move in two directions, usually up-down and left-right. 3-axis robots add a rotational movement, typically for picking up items from various angles. 4-axis robots introduce another rotational axis, allowing for more precise movements and pick-and-place operations. 5-axis robots offer even greater flexibility with added tilt and swivel capabilities. 6-axis robots, the most advanced, have all previous axes plus the ability to rotate around the tool itself. Other markets may include specialty robots for unique applications.</p></p>
