<p><p>Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) is a type of fuel produced from non-recyclable waste materials. There are two main types of RDF markets: Dense RDF and Loose RDF. Dense RDF is made up of compacted and baled waste materials, which are easier to transport and store. Loose RDF consists of shredded waste materials that are less compact and may require different handling and storage methods. Both types of RDF can be used as an alternative fuel source in various industries.</p></p>
